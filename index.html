Regeneration is a measured process, not a metaphor

Regeneration is not a metaphor borrowed from nature. It is a measured process that natural systems run after catastrophic disturbance. A serotinous pine cone opens only under the heat of a fire, releasing seed into ground the fire has cleared. The fire is the condition the forest evolved to require. A kelp forest flattened by a storm regrows into a structure suited to the conditions that now hold, not the ones that came before.

Stuart J. Green spent thirty years studying these systems as a marine scientist before applying their logic to organisations. An institution under compound disruption is not waiting to return to a prior state. It is being asked to release dormant capacity, and to regrow into a structure that fits what it now faces. That translation, from marine systems science to institutional strategy, is the work.

Questions leaders ask

What is an operating model misfit?

An operating model misfit is the gap between how an organisation is structured to work and the conditions it now faces. The structure was built for conditions that no longer hold, so effort produces less than it should and the organisation strains to maintain results.

Why is AI exposing operating models?

AI did not break the operating model. It revealed a model that had already stopped fitting. AI raises the baseline of what is possible, which makes the existing structure's limits visible and measurable rather than hidden.

What is the Regenerate framework?

The Regenerate framework is a methodology for organisations whose previous model is structurally obsolete and where recovery to a prior state is not an option. It runs in three phases, Raze, Enrich and Grow, drawn from how natural systems regenerate after catastrophic disturbance.

What is the difference between resilience and regeneration?

Resilience aims to restore a prior state and optimises for stability. Regeneration assumes the prior state is gone and redesigns the system for the conditions that now hold. Resilience looks backward; regeneration looks forward.
